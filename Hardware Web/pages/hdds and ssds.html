HDDs - Hard Disk Drives

Used for long-term storage of files and programs on a computer, including operating systems, software, and documents.
Generally have read/write speeds ranging from 80 to 160 MB/s.
2.5-inch, also known as small form factor, and 3.5-inch, or large form factor.
Non-volatile storage devices, meaning they retain data even when the computer is turned off.
A type of secondary storage device used in computers to store data and programs permanently.

SSDs - Solid State Drives

Used in most PCs at the moment as they are much faster and more reliable.
They use flash memory similar to USB sticks to store data and do not use mechanical parts so are silent.
Much faster than HDDs. They offer quick boot times, fast file transfers, and improved system responsiveness. They also use less power.
There are several different connections you can use with SSDs such as SATA, NVMe (M.2 or PCIe) and External with a USB connection.
They are limited to a number of read and write cycles per second though but most users won't notice this.
SATA has ~500–550 MB/s as read and ~450–500 MB/s write.
NVMe with PCIe 5.0 or later can have ~10,000–14,000 MB/s read and ~10,000–12,000 MB/s write.
